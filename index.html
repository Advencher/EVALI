What is EVALI?

EVALI (E-cigarette or Vaping product use-Associated Lung Injury) is a serious respiratory illness linked to the use of e-cigarettes and vaping products. First identified in 2019, this condition can cause severe lung damage and even death in some cases.

The condition is characterized by symptoms that may include:

  • Shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Cough
  • Fever and chills
  • GI symptoms like nausea, vomiting, and diarrhea
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Fatigue

EVALI often requires hospitalization and in severe cases, patients may need intensive care support including mechanical ventilation (breathing machines). While vitamin E acetate has been strongly linked to EVALI, research continues to investigate other potential contributing factors and chemicals involved.

Health Risks Associated with EVALI

EVALI can cause severe and potentially life-threatening damage to the lungs and respiratory system. Below are the key health risks associated with this condition:

Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS)

EVALI can lead to ARDS, a serious condition where fluid builds up in the air sacs of the lungs, preventing oxygen from getting to the bloodstream. This can require mechanical ventilation and intensive care support.

Permanent Lung Damage

Even after recovery, many patients experience reduced lung function and scarring (pulmonary fibrosis) that can persist for months or years. Some damage may be irreversible, leading to chronic breathing problems.

Secondary Infections

Damaged lungs are more susceptible to bacterial and viral pneumonia, which can complicate recovery and lead to additional health problems.

Hypoxemia and Organ Damage

Severe cases of EVALI can result in dangerously low blood oxygen levels (hypoxemia), which can damage other vital organs like the heart, brain, and kidneys due to insufficient oxygen supply.

Mortality Risk

EVALI can be fatal. According to CDC data, approximately 2-3% of confirmed EVALI cases have resulted in death, with higher risks for patients with underlying health conditions.

Long-Term Health Consequences

Research is ongoing, but preliminary studies suggest that EVALI survivors may face increased risks of chronic respiratory conditions, reduced exercise capacity, and other long-term health issues.

The CDC and FDA strongly recommend avoiding the use of all e-cigarette or vaping products, particularly those containing THC and especially those from informal sources like friends, family, or in-person or online dealers.

Resources and Further Information

If you or someone you know uses e-cigarettes or vaping products and develops symptoms consistent with EVALI, seek medical attention immediately. Early intervention can significantly improve outcomes.

Symptoms Requiring Immediate Medical Attention:

  • Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • Chest pain
  • Severe coughing
  • Coughing up blood

Prevention:

The most effective way to prevent EVALI is to avoid the use of all e-cigarette and vaping products. If you currently vape and want to quit, talk to a healthcare provider about evidence-based cessation strategies.

For Healthcare Providers:

The CDC provides clinical guidance for the diagnosis and management of EVALI. Consider EVALI in patients with respiratory symptoms who report e-cigarette or vaping product use.
